    The LaSallian General Elections Special

Lasallians on the General Elections

The month of March signals the beginning of the General Elections (GE) period, characterized by non-stop room-to-room campaigns and the sea of students donning orange and yellow. The University’s two political parties Alyansang Tapat sa Lasalista (Tapat) and Santugon sa Tawag ng Panahon (Santugon) use this season to communicate their party’s platforms and their slate’s credentials to Lasallian voters. In contrast, we wanted to hear what the voters have to say about Lasallian politics - and what we heard was compelling.

TAPAT & SANTUGON

Positions on Student Representation, Tuition Increase and Other Issues

Year in, year out, the DLSU community faces problems both new and recurring that require continuous discourse between the University’s multiple sectors in order to arrive at solutions and devise plans of action. As the University now enters into the General Election season, the two political parties, Alyansang Tapat sa Lasalista and Santugon sa Tawag ng Panahon, weigh in on these various issues where students might be concerned.
